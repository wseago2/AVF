Discuss: Industry Research


  • What are the “top selling” app categories in the United States?

    Games, entertainment media and education.


    What are the “top selling” app categories in Japan?

    Utilities, entertainment media and games.


    What are the “top selling” app categories in Great Britain?

    Games, navigation utilities and entertainment media


  • Based on the three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

    Games and entertainment media definitely rule mobile device use on a global scale. This trend is driven by users accessing devices during microbreaks, and by the increased availability of high speed access coupled with unlimited data.


  • What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets?

    In the US, high speed data plans start at $30 per month for capped service. Unlimited plans start at $50 per month.


    In the UK, lack of competition for high speed providers has resulted in capped service only. Rates begin as low as $12 monthly for 3G sim card service only, and $26 monthly for handset included service. Bandwidth limits are much more strict in the UK, with the average 4G connected service costing $61 per month, with a 20GB limit cap.


    Japan has a public WiFi network that can be accessed for about $5 per month. The data is unlimited, but the coverage is spotty at best. Reliable ultra high speed service is available for about the same cost as in the US.


  • How may this impact development?

    As developers, we need to always keep bandwidth usage in mind. Applications that consume extreme amounts of data will cause a consumer to discontinue using the app, no matter how well it fills a need.


  • What would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice?

    In light of the information gained from this research, I would have to say that a game app that would appeal to users during microbreaks would have the greatest chance to deliver a profit.
